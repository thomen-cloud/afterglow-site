The short version

Afterglow has no accounts and no tracking. Your photos, symptom logs, notes, and

reports live on your phone. Exactly three kinds of network requests can ever leave

the app, and two of them carry no personal data at all.

What stays on your phone

Everything, by default: your recovery photos (full resolution), your symptom

check-ins, your notes, your provider's name and phone number if you saved them,

and every provider report you generate. There is no account, no cloud backup by

us, and no analytics or advertising SDK in the app.

The three things that can leave your phone

A photo you choose to send for an AI Photo Note. Only when you tap the

consent button, only that one photo, with location metadata stripped first.

It travels through our relay (which holds the API key and stores nothing) to

OpenRouter, which routes it to OpenAI's API to write the note. We do not store

your photo on any server. OpenRouter processes the request per its privacy

terms (prompt logging is disabled for our account); OpenAI's API may retain

API inputs briefly for abuse monitoring per its API data-usage policy, and

does not train on API data. If you never tap the consent button, no photo

ever leaves your phone.

Your email address, if you ask for daily cards by email. Optional,

write-only, used solely to send the cards you asked for. [Feature not yet

live; this section activates with it.]

A feature-availability check. The app fetches one small public

configuration file so we can switch the AI feature off remotely if we ever

need to. The request carries no personal data.

Purchases are processed by Apple; we never see your payment details.

Deleting your data

Settings → "Delete all my data" permanently removes everything from your phone:

photos and thumbnails, symptom logs, notes, AI Photo Notes, saved provider

reports, your provider's contact details, capture-calibration files, and pending

notifications. There is no server copy for us to delete — nothing exists to

recall. One honest limit: anything you already exported or shared (a report you

AirDropped, photos you saved to your camera roll or sent to someone) is outside

the app and can't be un-shared by us.

AI Photo Notes are not medical advice

Notes describe what a photo appears to show, in plain words. They are not a

diagnosis or medical assessment, and the app never analyzes, measures, or scores

your skin. Your provider's instructions always come first.
